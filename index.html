The Workflow: Moving past dot phrases to prompts

The project's goal is simple: take the unstructured paragraphs from an AI scribe and, using a series of prompts, instantly convert them into customized clinical notes directly within the EMR.

1. AI Scribe Captures Visit

The AI Scribe listens to the patient encounter and generates its best first draft.

2. EMR LLM Applies Prompts

The clinician triggers one of three custom-designed prompts inside the EMR's internal LLM.

3. Structured Output Generated

The LLM returns precisely formatted, ready-to-use content, saving time and reducing cognitive load.

The Three Core Prompts

Each prompt is designed to perform one task exceptionally well, making the system modular and easy to refine.

Prompt 1

A/P Formatting ("Pithy")

Transforms verbose paragraphs into scannable, problem-oriented Assessment & Plan notes using concise clinical shorthand. It's the best :P

Before: Raw Scribe Output

Patient presents with asthma. We discussed starting Flovent 44mcg, two puffs twice a day. The family should continue to use the albuterol inhaler as needed. We also went over proper spacer technique. They will return to the clinic in three months or sooner if needed.

After: Formatted Output

Asthma - Flovent 44mcg 2 puff BID started - Continue albuterol PRN - Use spacer - RTC 3mo/PRN
Prompt 2

Billing Analysis

Assesses the Medical Decision Making (MDM) components of a note based on 2021 E/M guidelines. It suggests an appropriate CPT code and provides a clear, traceable audit trail.

Example Output:

Problems: Moderate (Acute illness with systemic symptoms) Data: Low (Independent historian) Risk: Moderate (prescription antibiotic) MDM Score: Problems (Moderate) + Data (Low) + Risk (Moderate) = Moderate (based on 2 of 3) Final Code: 99214
Prompt 3

AVS Generation

Creates a personalized After Visit Summary (AVS) with three warm, context-aware sign-off options and a simple, actionable to-do list for the family.

Example Output:

Great seeing you guys today! Good luck with soccer and kindergarten! To-Do List: Tests/Results: - Strep test: Results pending, we will call tomorrow Appointments: - Return to clinic if no improvement in 2-3 days

Core Prompt Design Principles

This project was built on three key insights discovered through extensive testing and iteration.

1. Few-Shot Examples are Critical

Showing the LLM exactly what you want with clear examples is far more effective than just telling it with instructions. Examples are the cornerstone of reliable, high-quality output.

2. Brevity Improves Quality

For clinical notes, shorter is better. Extremely concise, shorthand bullets are faster for clinicians to scan and approve, requiring less editing and feeling more natural.

3. Separate Prompts for Separate Functions

Unbundling tasks into single-purpose prompts (one for formatting, one for billing) makes the system more robust, easier to maintain, and allows for independent use.
